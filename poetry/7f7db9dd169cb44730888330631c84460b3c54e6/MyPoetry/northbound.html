Northbound

brian beckage


22 September 2024

A car northbound in the southbound lane
shoots past us in the dark
in the passing lane
where we were hurtling south just moments ago.
Nothing changes in the material world,
no crumpled metal
no broken bodies
scattered across the asphalt,
no long lines of traffic
backed up for miles in the night
as emergency workers clean up the mess.
No, none of that, nothing at all.
But everything changes
A reminder, a reinforcement, a renewed appreciation
that we are like fluff from a cottonwood tree
floating in the wind in early summer
random or not
not knowing when or where we'll land,
an end and a beginning.
